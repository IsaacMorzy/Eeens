  ## What moved

  In March 2026 a distribution operator took over a 9,000 sq ft godown at Syokimau, KM 11. They had
  been in our Mlolongo unit for six years before that. They moved for one reason: their truck-court
  access at the old site had a 14 m turning radius and the new owner of the neighbouring plot had
  started parking 40-foot containers in the shared access lane. They were one flat-tyre incident
  away from a service-call escalation with their biggest customer.

  We had the Syokimau unit surveyed in February. The truck-court radius there is 21 m, which is
  short of the 24 m a 40-foot side-loader wants but workable with a top-loader driver who knows the
  approach. We published the radius as part of the spec sheet for that unit and the operator was the
  one who called to ask about it.

  They signed a three-year standard lease at the published KSH per sqft. They moved on a Sunday with
  one truck. They were operating by Tuesday evening.

  ## What changed for them

  Three months in, the operator reported the following:

  - Load times per truck dropped by an average of 22 minutes. The 21 m radius plus the absence of
    neighbour containers means drivers can do a continuous loop instead of a shunting approach.
  - One driver out of four left. The other three absorbed the workload comfortably. The operator
    did not replace the fourth. We don't have visibility into why and they didn't tell us.
  - They are now running an extra half-shift on weekdays. The unit's column-grid allows three racking
    levels where the Mlolongo unit allowed two, and the picking throughput per shift increased by
    roughly a third.

  The operator told us in May that they would renew at the end of the three-year term. We did not
  ask them to tell us this. They volunteered it during a site walk.

  ## What changed for us

  Two receipts.

  First: we under-estimated how much corridor migration happens when truck-court access on a
  neighbouring plot changes hands. The Mlolongo operator's move was triggered by something we did
  not control, on a property we did not own. We assumed Syokimau would get a different sort of
  tenant. Instead it got a second-generation tenant who came with their racking plan from day one.

  Second: the published truck-court radius on the spec sheet is doing real work. Of the operators
  who have asked about the Syokimau unit since publication, four out of seven have asked specifically
  about the radius. We did not predict that ratio. We have not yet updated the Mlolongo listings
  to publish their radius, but we are going to.

  ## What we are not changing

  The 3-year standard lease term. Operators who have done a multi-shift move like this one have
  earned the right to sign for three years; the rent isn't going to spike mid-term because we
  published the truck-court radius and demand went up. The lease is the lease.
