  ## Three of our four zones are on one corridor. The fourth is not.

  Mlolongo and Syokimau share the Mombasa Road. KM 11 is Syokimau, KM 12 to 14 is Mlolongo. The drive
  between the two at 06:30 on a working day is about 9 minutes and 3 km. Baba Dogo is on Outering
  Road, which intersects Mombasa Road at the Jogoo Road interchange; the drive from Mlolongo to Baba
  Dogo at the same time is closer to 28 minutes and 17 km, depending on the Eastern Bypass choice.
  Thika is on the A2, north-east, and the drive from Mombasa Road KM 14 to Flame Tree Park Estate at
  06:30 is roughly 1 hour 22 minutes and 49 km.

  ## Why this matters when comparing listings

  An operator who needs both a logistics hub and a small parts-storage room treats KM 11 and KM 14 as
  the same patch. They treat KM 14 and Baba Dogo as adjacent. They treat KM 14 and Thika as a
  separate planning exercise.

  When the listing does not say "Mombasa Road KM 14", an operator has to triangulate from the
  address, the zone, and the photo. We treat this as friction we owe the operator, and we publish
  the KM in the address line on every listing, in mono, so that the operator can do the math
  themselves.

  ## What the corridor gives you that an amenity list does not

  The Mombasa Road corridor between Syokimau and Athi River has:

  - A KPLC industrial feeder with documented redundancy on three of the four substations serving
    KM 11 to KM 14.
  - A Nairobi Water trunk main at Mlolongo, sized for industrial demand, that does not pass through
    the residential pressure zones upstream.
  - A rail siding at the Inland Container Depot, KM 13. The siding is operated by KRC and is
    available by application for bonded cargo. The container dwell time before our 2024 re-survey
    was 11 days; the 2026 figure is 6 days.
  - A 24-hour traffic-patrolled segment from KM 8 to KM 16, which the operator in the previous post
    noted affects overnight dispatch timing.

  None of these are on a `/amenities` page anywhere. They are not amenity items. They are operational
  infrastructure, and they are why the corridor has been continuously leased for industrial use
  since the 1980s. The amenity lists published by some of our competitors focus on the building:
  gym, food court, parking ratio. Our listings focus on what the building connects to.

  ## On Thika

  Thika is not on Mombasa Road. It is north, up the A2. The drive is 1 hour 22 minutes and the
  residential market is a different buyer profile (owner-occupier 3-bedroom apartments). Listing a
  Thika apartment on the same filter rail as a Mlolongo warehouse would be misleading by category,
  so we don't. The /properties filter has a zone selector that pulls Thika out separately, and the
  /locations page describes the four zones as four different reasons to pick one.
